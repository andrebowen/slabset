How it's worked out

Concrete is sold by volume in cubic metres (m³). Measure the space you are filling, work out its volume, add wastage, then convert to bags or a premix order.

Volume by shape

Slab / footing  =  length × width × thickness
Cylinder (column / post)  =  π × (diameter ÷ 2)² × height

For slabs, length and width default to metres and thickness stays in millimetres. Footings and cylinders use millimetres throughout. Pouring more than one identical item? Multiply by the quantity.

Wastage

Real sites lose concrete to spillage, an uneven sub-base and over-dug holes. 10% is standard. Bump it up for rough ground or drop it for tidy formwork. Your total to order is volume × (1 + wastage%).

Bags or premix

A 20 kg bag yields about 9.8 litres (0.0098 m³), so roughly 102 bags fill a cubic metre. We round bags up. Premix from a truck is sold in 0.1 m³ steps. The Best buy line compares both options. Under about 0.5 m³ many plants charge a short-load fee, so bags often win.

Steel reinforcement

Turn on reinforcement to size the steel. Slabs use standard 6.0 × 2.4 m mesh sheets with 10% for laps, plus bar chairs at roughly 1.5 per m². Footings use trench mesh in 6 m lengths with a 15% lap allowance. Always defer to your engineer's drawings for anything structural.

Concrete FAQs

How many 20 kg bags of concrete are in a cubic metre?

About 102 bags. A 20 kg bag yields roughly 0.0098 m³ (9.8 litres), so 1 ÷ 0.0098 ≈ 102. Always round up and keep a spare bag or two on hand.

How thick should a concrete slab be?

A typical domestic path or shed slab is 100 mm. Driveways that take cars are usually 100–125 mm. Heavier loads go to 150 mm or more. Check council and engineering requirements for anything structural.

Should I use bags or order premix?

Under about 0.5 m³, bags are easiest. Above that, premix from a truck is cheaper per cubic metre and far less work. This calculator costs both so you can compare.

How much extra concrete should I allow for wastage?

10% is the standard allowance for spillage, an uneven sub-base and over-dug footings. On rough ground or hand-dug holes, 15% is safer. Tidy formwork on a level base can drop to 5%.

How much reo mesh do I need for a slab?

Reo mesh comes in 6.0 × 2.4 m sheets (14.4 m²). Divide your slab area by 14.4 and add about 10% for overlaps. A 15 m² slab needs 2 sheets. Turn on reinforcement below to get sheets, bar chairs and an indicative cost.

How much does a cubic metre of concrete weigh?

About 2.4 tonnes (2400 kg).
